Column-Family Databases

Column-Family (or Column-Oriented) databases are designed to handle huge amounts of data. Some say they have their origins at Google when the company published a paper called BigTable, while others argue that Column-Families pre-date the BigTable paper. Most Column-Family databases have the following characteristics:

Column-Family Characteristics

  • Data from a given column (in a two-dimensional table) are logically related
  • Each row can have a different set of columns (or none at all)
  • They can scale (or grow) on clusters of commodity hardware---if you need more storage, you just add more--often inexpensive--hardware

Popular Column-Family Implementations

There are lots of different Column-Family databases, but three of the most popular are:
  • Apache Hbase
  • Apache Cassandra
  • Hypertable
